Kisir

Kisir is a beloved traditional Turkish salad made from fine bulgur wheat, and it holds a special place in almost every Turkish household. For me, it's more than just a salad — it's a symbol of togetherness, often prepared for family gatherings, tea times, or when friends come over for an afternoon chat. It's made with fine bulgur, tomato and pepper paste, olive oil, lemon juice, and plenty of fresh herbs like parsley and mint. I love how its flavors are both tangy and slightly spicy — a true reflection of Turkish cuisine. In my hometown, we often serve kisir with lettuce leaves and a cup of tea when friends visit. To me, it's a taste of home and shared moments around the table.

Baklava

Baklava is the most popular Turkish dessert, known for its golden layers of flaky pastry, crushed pistachios, and sweet syrup. Contrary to popular belief, most people in Türkiye don't actually know how to make it — but in my hometown, Gaziantep, baklava is part of everyday life. You can find it in almost every bakery, freshly baked and perfectly crisp. I grew up surrounded by its rich aroma, and for me, baklava isn't just a dessert; it's a proud symbol of my city's culinary heritage and craftsmanship.

About Me

I'm originally from Gaziantep in Turkiye, where many civilizations lived during centuries. Growing up surrounded by rich flavors and stories of the past inspired my passion for cooking. In my blog, I share recipes that blend traditional Gaziantep cuisine with modern techniques, always keeping authenticity at heart. Each dish tells a story — of family, culture, and the joy of sharing food.
